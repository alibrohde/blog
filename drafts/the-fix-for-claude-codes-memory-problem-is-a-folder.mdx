For years, all my notes lived in Notion, with a side flirtation with Google Docs. It worked fine for me. It didn't work for Claude Code.

I wrote about this asymmetry back when I first started playing with CC: it could read my file system, not my Notion workspace. Notion's MCP has narrowed the gap but not closed it. Remote still means API latency and auth headaches. Local is just files on disk, always there, and that was enough to change my calculus.

So I moved everything to [Obsidian](https://obsidian.md), a local-first markdown editor that stores notes as plain `.md` files on disk. And the problem dissolved.

## 1,204 Files, Suddenly Readable

Four years of daily journal entries. Research notes. Deal memos. Transcripts from every episode of my podcast, [10 Minutes or Less](https://www.youtube.com/@10minutesorless). All of it sitting in a single folder on my laptop.

Analyses that used to be impossible became trivial. Joining years of sleep data against mood scores from my journals? Point CC at the folder, ask the question, done. Pulling every founder I've mentioned across two years of deal memos into a structured list? Same.

The shift wasn't about finding a better editor. It was about giving the model something it could actually read.

## The Upkeep Problem Solved Itself

Obsidian has always asked something of its users: link your notes, organize your folders, keep naming conventions consistent. That's exactly the work I was never going to do by hand. It's also exactly the work Claude Code now does for free.

CC writes the notes. CC names the files. CC links them together. CC even writes and evolves my `claude.md`, the instruction file at the root of the vault that tells it how I work.

The thing that kept most of us in Notion (it's prettier, it's easier, it doesn't demand discipline) got inverted. Obsidian's "demands" are now just work the AI does.

## Pre-AI Cost High. Post-AI Cost Zero.

This is why I think Obsidian is genuinely reaccelerating, not just riding a vibe.

AI makes it easier to use: Claude Code handles the upkeep I was never going to sustain.

AI makes it more valuable: a local markdown vault is exactly the substrate every AI session can read.

Pre-AI, the cost of using Obsidian well was high, which is why most of us defaulted to Notion or Docs. Post-AI, the cost is zero and the payoff compounds with every note you write.

## Context Is the Real Product

Context is what makes Claude Code useful. But nobody wants to re-explain every project every session, and nobody wants their `claude.md` stuffed with every detail.

The vault holds the context. `claude.md` just tells CC where to look.

That separation is what unlocks it. One file of instructions. A whole folder of lived experience. The model walks the folder when it needs to.
